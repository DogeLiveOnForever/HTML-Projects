How To Care For Guinea Pigs

The Steps On How To Handle Guinea Pigs

1.) In order to handle a guinea pig, you have to first let him/her get use to you first, if you just bought them from the pet store.

2.) Put a towel on your lap, in case he/she decides it's a good time to pee or poop on you. (Trust me, it has happened many times...)

3.) Next, you want to reach for him/her and slide on hand under the guinea pig and and the other around. Make sure your being gentle, but at the same time, firm.

4.) Make sure you are calm, or they will start to try to get away from you. Pet them gently so you don't hurt them!

5.) Finally, when putting your guinea pig back in the cage, put them "back first", so they don't try to hop out of your hand and land in the cage, hurting their back.

Guinea Pig Sounds And What They Mean

Though guinea pigs seem like quiet creatures, they use noises quite often to express themselves. Understanding these is the key to understanding your critter companion.

Sound Meaning Good/Bad
Whistling They are excited. It maybe feeding or play time. Good
Purring This can mean two different things. If it's a low, relaxed purr, than he/she is content. If they make a high pitched, short purr, they are in fear or are annoyed. Good or Bad
Hissing This sound may also sound like chattering teeth and is a sign that your pet guinea pig is angry or aggressive. It is often accompanied by bared teeth. Bad
Chriping One strange guinea pig sound you may observe is a chirping noise, often made when your guinea pig is in a trance-like state. It may occur when a guinea pig loses a mate. ???
